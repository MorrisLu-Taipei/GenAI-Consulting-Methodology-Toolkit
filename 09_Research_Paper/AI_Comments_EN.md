### 2. The three attack faces reviewers are most likely to press (adversarial honesty)

Antigravity's supplement was forward-positive; Codex's was conservative but still closed with "deserves publication." As an actual polish-phase participant, I believe the **most worth-worrying** three attack faces are:

**Attack face A: N = 1 sample size.** The paper's entire empirical basis is the same single artifact (GenAI Consulting Methodology Toolkit). Hevner DSR permits N = 1 instantiation, but reviewers from more empirically oriented traditions (such as BPM Journal, Information Systems Research) will question "why believe this paradigm generalizes to other authors, other domains?" **Attack face B: Missing counterfactual baseline.** §4.2 has already softened the absolute claim that "single IDE doesn't work," but a reviewer may further ask: "If one used only Claude Code as a single IDE, also running `/devil-pair-debate` (spawning fresh sub-agents) and `/consistency-review`, how much worse would the outcome be?" **Attack face C: The payload methodology itself is not yet independently validated.** §9.4 marks this point, but it will still be magnified by reviewers -- because the paper claims this is a methodology artifact "worth producing via multi-IDE investment"; if future case-study results for the payload itself are negative or neutral, the entire production-paradigm's academic value will be questioned by association.
