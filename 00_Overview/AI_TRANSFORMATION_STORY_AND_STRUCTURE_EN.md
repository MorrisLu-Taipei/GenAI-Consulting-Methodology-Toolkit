## 1. Program Positioning

This program is not simply about selling AI courses, nor is it just about selling tool deployments.

The true positioning is:

> Help enterprises convert fragmented AI usage into organizational capability that is reproducible, governable, measurable, and scalable.

Most enterprises have already started using ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or other AI tools, but the majority remain stuck at individual experimentation. Employees find it useful, yet management sees no organizational benefit; some people produce results that cannot be replicated across the team; processes still rely on manual data transfer, copy-paste, sending emails, filling out forms, and updating systems.

Our strategy is to lift the enterprise up along a clear consulting pathway:

1. Use a simple questionnaire to diagnose the enterprise's current AI maturity.
2. Configure the L1-L5 course ratio based on the diagnostic result.
3. After training, use an eight-stage consulting methodology to produce an AI Transformation Diagnostic Report.
4. The report proposes an executable Skill, Workflow, Agent, and Agent Team Roadmap.

## 2. A Scenario Story the Customer Can Understand

### 2.1 The Story's Protagonist

Suppose the customer is a 300-person manufacturing or services enterprise.

The company has Sales, Customer Service, Operations, Finance, IT, HR, and Management departments. Every department has started hearing about AI, but the actual usage is highly inconsistent:

- Sales staff use ChatGPT to draft visit emails and presentations.
- The customer service manager wants to organize an FAQ, but the data is scattered across Gmail, CRM, Notion, and Excel.
- The operations manager wants to know the daily exception orders, but data exported from the ERP needs manual cleanup.
- The CEO wants to push AI, but only hears about isolated cases and cannot judge where the company truly stands.
- IT worries about security, permissions, data leakage, and the maintenance cost of system integration.

At this point, simply selling tools makes it hard for the customer to understand the value; and if you only deliver training, after the course people easily fall back into the original processes.

So what we want to tell is a transformation story:

> First understand where the company is today, then strengthen employee and departmental capabilities, and finally turn the truly valuable scenarios into a landable AI transformation roadmap.

### 2.2 Before: The Typical State Before Enterprise AI Adoption

In the beginning, enterprises usually do not have "no AI at all"; instead, "AI is already scattered everywhere."

Common situations:

- Some people are very skilled with AI, but the methods stay on their personal computers or in chat histories.
- Departments have no shared Prompts, SOPs, templates, or knowledge bases.
- Customer data sits in the CRM, tasks in Notion, spreadsheets in Google Sheets, emails in Gmail, orders in the ERP — none of them connected.
- When management asks about AI's ROI, the field can only answer "things seem to be faster."
- IT cannot judge which scenarios are ready for automation and which scenarios should be governed first.

This means enterprises are not lacking an AI tool — they are lacking a "maturity map" and a "transformation methodology."

### 2.3 Transformation: The Three-Stage Journey We Take Customers Through

#### Stage 1: Questionnaire Diagnostic

First, use a simple questionnaire to quickly identify which level the enterprise is currently at:

- L1: Employees individually use Chat AI.
- L2: Departments begin to consolidate Skills, SOPs, Prompts, and templates.
- L3: Begin using n8n to integrate systems and workflows.
- L4: Begin using Hermes Agent equipped with Wiki memory, tool calling, scheduling, evidence, and human Gates.
- L5: Multiple Agents form a team that collaboratively completes enterprise-class processes.

The purpose of the questionnaire is not academic measurement, but to give the CEO, managers, and IT a shared language about the current state.

#### Stage 2: Course-Based Capability Building

Courses are not a fixed menu; the ratio is configured based on the diagnostic result.

For example:

- If the customer is largely at L1, the curriculum emphasizes OpenWebUI, AI usage guidelines, Prompts, data security, and departmental Skill-ification.
- If the customer already has departmental templates and SOPs, the curriculum moves toward n8n Workflow and system integration.
- If the customer already has an automation foundation, you can add the Hermes Agent knowledge-work closed loop, and further evaluate the ClawTeam Agentic AI Team course.

The goal of the course is not just to teach tool operation, but to enable customers to produce their own work assets during the course:

- Prompt Library
- Skill list
- SOPs and templates
- Workflow PoC
- Hermes Agent task examples
- AI Team collaboration scenarios

Course design must also reference each level's requirement list and the company's profile. In other words, L1-L5 is not a fixed lesson plan but a maturity architecture; what is actually taught, what cases are used, and how deep we go depends on the customer's industry, data sensitivity, IT capability, existing systems, and deployment mode.

For example, the same L3 n8n:

- A marketing/services company may prioritize integrating Gmail, Sheets, Notion, and CRM, to rapidly produce client proposals and campaign reports.
- An R&D/manufacturing company may prioritize integrating ERP, internal APIs, databases, quality documents, and exception order processes.
- An enterprise with highly sensitive data may first go Hybrid or fully on-premises, plus add human review, permission control, and audit logs.

#### Stage 3: Consulting Diagnostic Report

After the course is not the end — it is the entry point into consulting diagnostic work.

We use an eight-stage methodology to organize:

1. Enterprise current state.
2. AI maturity level.
3. Process and data gaps.
4. High-value scenarios.
5. Priority deployment order.
6. Three-phase Roadmap.
7. ROI and governance recommendations.
8. Subsequent deployment plan.

The final deliverable is an AI Transformation Diagnostic Report that management can understand, department heads can endorse, IT can evaluate, and the team can execute.

### 2.4 After: The Change the Customer Should Feel

After completing the first round of services, the customer should be able to answer the following questions:

- What is our company's current AI maturity level?
- Which departments already have an AI-ready foundation?
- Which processes are best suited to be automated first?
- Which data and systems need to be integrated?
- For next quarter, should we focus on L2 Skill, L3 Workflow, L4 Agent, or L5 Agent Team?
- After pushing AI, which KPIs should we use to track effectiveness?

This is the transition from "everyone trying AI individually" to "the company has an AI transformation roadmap."

## 3. L1-L5 Maturity and Course Modules

### 3.1 L1 Chat AI: OpenWebUI

#### Positioning

OpenWebUI is the enterprise's internal AI conversation portal.

The focus at L1 is to enable employees to begin using AI safely, stably, and effectively to handle daily knowledge work.

#### Typical Scenario

A sales rep needs to write customer visit emails, meeting summaries, and proposal drafts. Where it used to take 1 hour to organize, OpenWebUI can now produce a first draft within 15 minutes, which the person then refines.

#### Value the Customer Will Understand

- Employees begin saving personal work time.
- The company can establish a unified AI portal.
- Models, permissions, and usage guidelines become easier to manage.
- Establishes the common foundation for subsequent Skill-ification and Workflow-ification.

#### Course Content

- OpenWebUI basic operation.
- AI conversation and Prompt fundamentals.
- Summarization, rewriting, translation, classification, brainstorming.
- Enterprise AI usage guidelines.
- Data and tasks that are not appropriate to hand to AI.

#### Course Deliverables

- Personal high-frequency work Prompts.
- Departmental AI usage notes.
- Common functional scenario examples.

### 3.2 L2 Skill AI: Antigravity / Claude Code / Codex

#### Positioning

The focus at L2 is to turn individual experience into departmental capability.

Antigravity, Claude Code, and Codex are not just for writing code — they serve as "Skill construction tools": consolidating a top performer's working method into reusable specifications, prompts, documents, templates, and operating procedures.

#### Typical Scenario

A marketing manager has always been great at writing product copy, but every time a new hire joins they have to teach again. Through L2 Skill AI, the copy generation process can be organized into a "Copy Generation Skill":

- Target audience.
- Tone and style.
- Prohibited expressions.
- Product selling points.
- Sample copy.
- Output format.

A new hire only needs to invoke the Skill and can produce content close to the department's standard.

#### Value the Customer Will Understand

- No longer dependent on individual heroes.
- Good methods can be replicated.
- New-hire onboarding time is shortened.
- Departmental quality becomes more consistent.
- Knowledge begins to accumulate as a company asset.

#### Course Content

- Definition and examples of a Skill.
- How to write personal experience into a Skill.
- How to consolidate Prompts, SOPs, Checklists, and Templates.
- How to use Antigravity / Claude Code / Codex to help produce documents and specifications.
- How to maintain a departmental Skill Library.

#### Course Deliverables

- Departmental Skill list.
- 3-5 high-frequency Skill examples.
- Skill standard template.
- Skill maintenance and version control recommendations.

### 3.3 L3 Workflow AI: n8n

#### Positioning

The focus at L3 is to make AI not just answer questions, but enter workflows, integrate systems, and complete tasks.

n8n is the core of Workflow AI, used to connect Gmail, Google Sheets, Notion, CRM, API, ERP, and other systems.

#### Typical Scenario

The customer service manager has to read Gmail complaint emails every day, look up customer data in the CRM, update the status in Google Sheets, and write a weekly report in Notion. This work is time-consuming and prone to missing data.

With n8n, you can design a Workflow:

1. Gmail receives a complaint email.
2. AI determines the complaint type and urgency.
3. Query the CRM for the customer's tier and history.
4. Query the ERP for order or shipment status.
5. Write the consolidated result into Google Sheets.
6. Automatically create a handling task in Notion.
7. Send an Email or Slack notification to the owner.
8. Automatically compile the customer service weekly report on Friday.

#### Value the Customer Will Understand

- Reduce repetitive manual data shuffling.
- Reduce missed orders, missed emails, and missed updates.
- Connect data flow, task flow, and decision flow.
- AI begins doing work for the company, not just chatting with people.

#### Scope of System Integration

- Gmail: email retrieval, classification, summarization, reply drafts.
- Google Sheets: data recording, list maintenance, report updates.
- Notion: task management, knowledge base, meeting notes.
- CRM: customer data, opportunities, interaction logs, service records.
- API: data exchange with internal and external systems.
- ERP: orders, inventory, shipments, procurement, financial data.
- Database: PostgreSQL, MySQL, vector databases, etc.
- Communication tools: Slack, Teams, LINE, Telegram.

#### Course Content

- n8n basic concepts.
- Trigger, Node, Credential, Webhook.
- Gmail / Sheets / Notion integration.
- API integration and data transformation.
- ERP / CRM integration planning.
- AI Node and RAG node applications.
- Error handling and human-review nodes.

#### Course Deliverables

- 1-2 departmental Workflow PoCs.
- Workflow scenario inventory table.
- System integration requirements list.
- Automation risk and permission checklist.

### 3.4 L4 Auto Agentic AI: Hermes Agent

#### Positioning

The focus at L4 is to upgrade AI from "a one-time answer" to "a continuously running knowledge-work agent." It not only understands tasks, decomposes steps, calls tools, and reports back results, but also retains memory, indexes, evidence, schedules, and human review records.

Hermes Agent is the standard platform for L4. It integrates L1 Chat, L2 Skill, and L3 Workflow into a knowledge-grade Agent operating system:

1. Markdown Wiki serves as the source of knowledge truth.
2. SQLite / FTS serves as the query index and derived cache.
3. Five core skills handle management, source analysis, keyword extraction, autonomous discovery, and briefing.
4. Deterministic tools handle writing, indexing, validation, extraction, caching, and logging.
5. Schedules drive nightly ingest, morning briefing, discovery ping, evening preview, and weekly graph synthesis.
6. Human Gates handle high-risk updates, keyword approval, schema changes, deletions, and go-live judgments.

#### Typical Scenario

The operations manager tells the AI assistant:

> Help me organize this week's exception orders, identify the top three root causes, produce a management meeting report, and list customers that need follow-up.

Hermes Agent will:

1. First read the department's `purpose.md`, `SCHEMA.md`, `INBOX.md`, `queue.md`, `watchlist.md`, and recent logs.
2. Call the ERP to query exception orders.
3. Call the CRM to query customer data.
4. Call Google Sheets to organize the analysis spreadsheet.
5. Call the L2 "Exception Analysis Skill."
6. Call the L3 report-generation Workflow.
7. Write the analysis process, cited sources, follow-up tasks, and executive summary back into the Wiki.
8. In the briefing, remind managers of which items require human confirmation.

#### Value the Customer Will Understand

- Staff don't need to know how each system operates.
- AI can help decompose and execute cross-system tasks.
- Managers can obtain decision data more quickly.
- AI transforms from a tool into a work agent that accumulates knowledge, traces evidence, and operates on a schedule.
- The Agent is not a one-shot worker — it organizes, reminds, updates, and produces briefings continuously, every day.

#### Course Content

- Agentic AI fundamentals and the L4 / L5 boundary.
- Hermes Agent architecture: Wiki, SQLite, skills, tools, runtime schema, policy.
- Orient-first SOP: Before working, the Agent first reads schema, purpose, INBOX, index, and log.
- Ingest / query / update / lint / graph-synthesis.
- Source analysis, keyword extraction, autonomous discovery, briefing-generator.
- How to design Agent-callable Skills and n8n Workflows.
- Human review, permissions, Logs, fallback, and Stage Gates.

#### Course Deliverables

- 1 departmental Hermes Agent task example.
- L4 IPOE table.
- Agent task specification.
- Initial Wiki: `purpose.md`, `SCHEMA.md`, INBOX, queue, watchlist, tasks.
- Agent-available tools list.
- Ingest / query / update test records.
- Briefing template.
- Gate 4A-4E acceptance table.
- Agent risk, permissions, and operations Runbook.

### 3.5 L5 Agentic Team AI: ClawTeam

#### Positioning

The focus at L5 is to have multiple specialist Agents collaborate, forming an AI team that completes enterprise-class tasks that a single Agent cannot.

ClawTeam is the landing platform for Agentic Team AI.

#### Typical Scenario

The CEO says:

> We want to develop a new product. Help me organize market opportunities, competitors, product positioning, marketing strategy, customer-service preparation, and financial projections.

ClawTeam can launch multiple Agents:

- Market Analysis Agent: analyzes market trends, customer segments, and competitors.
- Product Planning Agent: organizes product requirements and feature priorities.
- Marketing Strategy Agent: proposes channels, messaging, and campaign plans.
- Customer Service Agent: prepares FAQs, service workflows, and knowledge base.
- Financial Analysis Agent: estimates costs, revenue, budget, and ROI.
- Project Integration Agent: integrates all outputs into a project plan.

#### Value the Customer Will Understand

- Not one person plus one AI, but one department plus one AI team.
- Cross-departmental complex tasks can be drafted faster.
- Management can use the AI Team to prepare decision work upstream.
- The enterprise begins to acquire automated operations and innovation capabilities.

#### Course Content

- Multi-Agent collaboration concepts.
- Agent role design.
- Task assignment and integration.
- Role conflicts and quality checks.
- Agent Team output formats.
- Governance and supervision of enterprise-class tasks.

#### Course Deliverables

- An Agent Team scenario design.
- Agent role cards.
- Multi-Agent collaboration flow diagram.
- Agent Team output samples.

## 4. Simple Diagnostic Questionnaire Design

### 4.1 Questionnaire Purpose

Within 10-15 minutes, the questionnaire must let the customer understand:

- Where the company's current AI maturity stands.
- Which capabilities already have a foundation.
- Which gaps obstruct the next stage.
- How the course ratio should be configured.
- Which topics the subsequent consulting diagnostic should focus on.

### 4.2 Questionnaire Dimensions

We recommend dividing the questionnaire into six dimensions:

| Dimension | What it judges |
| --- | --- |
| Tool Usage | Whether employees stably use AI tools |
| Knowledge Consolidation | Whether there are Prompts, SOPs, templates, Skill Library |
| Process Automation | Whether there is system integration and automated workflows |
| System Integration | Whether Gmail, Sheets, Notion, CRM, API, ERP can be invoked by workflows |
| Agent Application | Whether there is an AI Agent that can autonomously execute tasks |
| Governance & ROI | Whether there are permissions, security, performance metrics, and management systems |

### 4.3 Diagnostic Output

The questionnaire outputs after completion:

- Current maturity level: L1-L5.
- Main gaps: e.g., knowledge not consolidated, workflows not integrated, Agent lacking governance.
- Recommended course ratio: e.g., L1 20%, L2 30%, L3 30%, L4 15%, L5 5%.
- Recommended priority scenarios: e.g., automated classification of customer service emails, sales proposal generation, ERP exception-order analysis.
- Subsequent consulting diagnostic focus: e.g., process inventory, data permissions, ROI metrics.

## 5. Course-Ratio Configuration Logic

### 5.1 Configuration Principles

The course ratio is neither randomly chosen by the customer nor fixed by us.

Recommended logic:

- First judge current maturity based on the questionnaire.
- Then look at management's objectives: efficiency, cost, quality, revenue, governance.
- Then look at system conditions: whether Gmail, Sheets, Notion, CRM, ERP, and API are already in place.
- Then look at the company profile: industry, data sensitivity, IT capability, budget, cloud policy.
- Then look at the deployment mode: cloud AI, Hybrid, fully on-premises.
- Finally, decide the L1-L5 course ratio.

### 5.2 Company Profile and Deployment Mode

Before course design, you must first investigate which AI architecture suits the customer.

#### Cloud AI

Cheapest and fastest to launch. Suitable for enterprises with low data sensitivity that are mainly focused on copywriting, research, customer service, marketing, and general knowledge work. The course can emphasize L1 and L2; L3 first integrates with SaaS tools such as Gmail, Sheets, Notion, and CRM.

#### Hybrid: Cloud + On-Premises

The most pragmatic path for most enterprises. Non-sensitive tasks can use cloud models, while sensitive data, internal systems, and core processes remain on-premises. The course should emphasize data classification, n8n routing, Credentials, API, ERP integration, and human review.

#### Fully On-Premises

Suitable for R&D/manufacturing, finance, healthcare, government, or highly sensitive data scenarios. The course should emphasize the OpenWebUI private portal, on-premises models, internal RAG, ERP/API/DB integration, Logs, permissions, auditing, and change management.

### 5.3 Industry Fit

#### R&D / Manufacturing

Common systems are ERP, MES, PLM, QMS, internal databases, and document systems. AI scenarios should focus on SOP lookup, quality anomalies, complaint analysis, R&D document summaries, ERP exception orders, and process knowledge consolidation. Typically recommend Hybrid or fully on-premises.

#### Marketing / Services

Common systems are Gmail, Sheets, Notion, CRM, ad platforms, and social tools. AI scenarios should focus on proposals, copywriting, competitor analysis, customer meeting summaries, CRM updates, and campaign performance reports. Typically can launch quickly from Cloud AI or Hybrid.

#### Highly Sensitive Industries

For example, finance, healthcare, government, and critical departments of listed companies. The course should prioritize data classification, permissions, auditing, human review, and on-premises deployment. L4/L5 should first be positioned as decision support rather than fully autonomous decision-making.

### 5.4 Example Configurations

#### Scenario A: AI Beginner Enterprise

Current state: Most employees have not yet stably used AI.

Recommended ratio:

- L1 OpenWebUI: 40%
- L2 Skill AI: 35%
- L3 n8n: 20%
- L4 Hermes Agent: 5%
- L5 ClawTeam: 0%

Goal: First build a shared language and departmental Skills.

#### Scenario B: AI in Use, but Workflow Adoption Lagging

Current state: Employees can use AI, but results are scattered and workflows remain manual.

Recommended ratio:

- L1 OpenWebUI: 15%
- L2 Skill AI: 30%
- L3 n8n: 40%
- L4 Hermes Agent: 10%
- L5 ClawTeam: 5%

Goal: Chain Skills into Workflows, prioritize completing 1-2 PoCs.

#### Scenario C: Automation Foundation in Place, Wants to Deploy Agents

Current state: Already has n8n or system integration experience.

Recommended ratio:

- L1 OpenWebUI: 5%
- L2 Skill AI: 20%
- L3 n8n: 25%
- L4 Hermes Agent: 30%
- L5 ClawTeam: 20%

Goal: Upgrade from process automation to Agent and Agent Team.

## 6. The Eight-Stage Consulting Diagnostic Method

### Stage 1: As-Is Snapshot — Current State Discovery

Inventory the enterprise's existing AI usage, systems, processes, data, and decision-making methods.

Outputs:

- Current state assessment report.
- As-Is Process Maps.
- AI usage inventory table.

### Stage 2: Reference Model Alignment — Standard Model Introduction

Introduce the L1-L5 maturity model so management and departments share a common language.

Outputs:

- Maturity assessment matrix.
- L1-L5 capability gap list.

### Stage 3: Best Practice Integration — Benchmarking Against Best Practices

Compare against common AI application patterns and benchmark cases in the industry.

Outputs:

- Industry benchmark scenario list.
- High-value AI application scenarios.

### Stage 4: Gap Analysis

Conduct a structured comparison between current state and target state, classifying as Missing, Broken, Redundant.

Outputs:

- Gap matrix.
- Impact x Effort priority ranking.

### Stage 5: Problem Definition — Core Problem Definition

Use the 80/20 principle to identify the problems most worth solving first.

Outputs:

- Executive Problem Statement.
- Key problems and root causes.

### Stage 6: Benchmarking and Phased Goals

Set short-, medium-, and long-term goals and Stage Gates.

Outputs:

- Three-phase goals.
- Acceptance criteria.
- KPI recommendations.

### Stage 7: To-Be Design — Future Blueprint Design

Design the future AI Operating Model.

Outputs:

- To-Be Operating Model.
- Skill Map.
- Workflow Map.
- Agent Map.
- System integration blueprint.

### Stage 8: Implementation and Change — Execution Deployment and Change Governance

Plan the deployment sequence, governance system, training plan, and ROI tracking.

Outputs:

- Transformation Roadmap.
- Change management plan.
- ROI tracking matrix.
- Follow-on deployment proposal.
