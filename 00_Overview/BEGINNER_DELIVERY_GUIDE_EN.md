## Don't try to hold all eight stages at once
On your first engagement, only remember the order of five things:

1. **Diagnose first** (never design a course or quote before diagnosis)
2. **Then design the course** (L1-L5 — fill the gap that's actually missing)
3. **Then produce the report** (every conclusion needs evidence)
4. **Then deliver and accept** (deliver an *acceptable capability*, not a tool)
5. **Then move to support or a quarterly review** (redraw the diagonal each quarter; did the structure really improve?)

> These five steps are the skeleton of the eight stages. Make the skeleton flow first; details later.

## The 5 mistakes beginners make
1. **Selling a full 24 months up front** — sell Phase A diagnosis first; let the Gate decide whether to continue.
2. **Designing a course before diagnosing** — without an L-level judgment, the course is always mis-fit.
3. **Mistaking a tool for transformation** — deploying a tool ≠ the client gaining capability; you deliver capability.
4. **A report with no evidence** — every conclusion must point to an interview quote, data, or observation.
5. **No Gate → scope creep** — Phase A/B/C need explicit sign-off exits, or it never ends and you never collect final payment.
