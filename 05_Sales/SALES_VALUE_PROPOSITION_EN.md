## 4. Role-Based Value Propositions

### 4.1 CEO

What the CEO cares about: whether AI investment can be turned into business outcomes.

What we provide:

- A clear, understandable AI maturity map.
- An objective assessment of where each department currently sits.
- Which AI scenarios have the strongest ROI.
- A three-phase Roadmap.
- Trackable KPIs and Stage Gates.

How we explain it to the CEO:

> This program will show you exactly where your company stands on AI today, where your next investment should go, and which scenarios are most likely to deliver results within 90 days.

### 4.2 COO

What the COO cares about: whether processes become faster, more stable, and have less rework.

What we provide:

- As-Is process inventory.
- Inventory of manual rework points.
- Integration scenarios for Gmail, Sheets, Notion, CRM, API, and ERP.
- n8n Workflow PoC.
- Cross-departmental process improvement recommendations.

How we explain it to the COO:

> We will move AI from being a personal chat tool to being part of real operating processes, prioritizing the most repetitive, time-consuming, and error-prone work.

### 4.3 CIO / IT

What the CIO / IT cares about: data security, access control, operations, audit, and system integration.

What we provide:

- Assessment of cloud AI / Hybrid / fully on-premise deployment.
- Inventory of credentials, APIs, access rights, and logs.
- Hermes Agent's available tools, Wiki memory, indexing, logs, and permission boundaries.
- Human review and exception handling mechanisms.
- Governance and operations recommendations.

How we explain it to CIO / IT:

> This is not about letting each department wire up tools on their own. It brings AI usage, process automation, and Agent permissions into a governable architecture.

### 4.4 HR / Training Function

What HR cares about: whether the way people work actually changes after the course is over.

What we provide:

- Pre-course maturity diagnosis.
- Course design segmented by department.
- Prompts, Skills, Workflows, and Agent task cards produced during the course.
- Post-course capability inventory.
- Departmental Skill Libraries and training tracking.

How we explain it to HR:

> This is not a one-off AI course; it turns training outcomes into reusable departmental work assets.

### 4.5 Department Heads

What department heads care about: whether the team can do less repetitive work, achieve more consistent quality, and onboard new hires faster.

What we provide:

- Inventory of the department's high-frequency tasks.
- Skill-ification.
- Workflow PoC.
- Hermes Agent task cards, briefings, and tracking lists.
- Departmental KPI recommendations.

How we explain it to department heads:

> We will first identify the work your department does most often, that takes the most time, and that is most error-prone, and convert it into Skills, Workflows, or Agent tasks.

## 5. 30-Second Sales Pitch

For most enterprises today, the issue is not that AI is unused. It is that everyone is using it on their own, results are scattered, processes are unchanged, and ROI is unclear. We first use a questionnaire to diagnose enterprise AI maturity, then configure the L1-L5 course mix accordingly, produce Prompts, Skills, Workflows, and Hermes Agent evidence during the course, and finally use an eight-stage consulting methodology to deliver an AI transformation diagnostic report so the enterprise knows where it stands, what to do next, and which processes can be automated first.

## 6. 3-Minute Sales Pitch

The most common state when enterprises adopt AI is that employees have already started using ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini, but the company as a whole sees no organizational capability. Some people can write Prompts, some organize data, some want to integrate with CRM or ERP, but there is no shared architecture.

We segment AI maturity into five levels. L1 is OpenWebUI Chat AI, which lets employees start using AI safely. L2 is Skill AI, which uses Antigravity, Claude Code, and Codex to turn personal experience into departmental Skills. L3 is n8n Workflow AI, which integrates Gmail, Sheets, Notion, CRM, API, and ERP, so that AI enters the process. L4 is Hermes Agent, which gives AI Wiki memory, the ability to absorb documents, call tools, generate briefings, and prove results with logs and evidence. L5 is ClawTeam, the AI Agent Team collaboration platform that supports cross-departmental tasks.

Our service has three phases. The first uses a questionnaire to rapidly diagnose which level the enterprise is at today. The second configures the course mix based on the diagnosis, so clients produce Prompts, Skills, Workflows, Hermes Agent PoCs, and Stage Gate evidence during training. The third uses the eight-stage consulting methodology to consolidate the current state, gaps, core issues, To-Be blueprint, Roadmap, and ROI.

What the client buys is not a single AI course, but a path that goes from AI learning to enterprise transformation implementation.

## 8. Common Objections

### Q1: We already have people using ChatGPT. Do we still need this?

Yes. Individuals using AI does not equal the company completing AI transformation. The difference lies in whether usage can be distilled into Skills, threaded into Workflows, brought under governance, and tracked against ROI.

### Q2: Our data cannot be on the cloud. Can we still do this?

Yes. The program first determines cloud AI, Hybrid, or fully on-premise deployment. Highly sensitive data can remain on-premise; lower-sensitivity tasks can use the cloud per policy.

### Q3: Once the course is over, is that it?

No. The course is only capability building. After the course, we move into the consulting diagnostic report, which delivers a Roadmap and PoCs that can be implemented.

### Q4: Will it be hard for IT to maintain?

We incorporate credentials, permissions, logs, human review, Owners, and operational responsibility into the design. Departments will not be left to wire things up on their own.

### Q5: How do we measure ROI?

We prioritize tracking time saved, error rate, response speed, process throughput, knowledge accumulation, new-hire ramp time, and PoC-to-production conversion rate.
